DATA LOADED
3-Axis Acceleration
100 Hz · X / Y / Z axes (g)
IMU
Accelerometer measures linear acceleration in 3 axes. The Y-axis stays near 1g (gravity), while X and Z show movement noise — typical of a wrist-worn device during walking.
Acceleration Magnitude
√(x²+y²+z²) · overall movement intensity
MAGNITUDE
The magnitude combines all 3 axes into a single activity signal. Values near 1g indicate standing still; spikes indicate sudden movement or steps.
3-Axis Angular Velocity
100 Hz · deg/s (dps)
GYRO
The gyroscope measures rotational velocity. The sinusoidal pattern in all axes reflects cyclic limb rotation — consistent with walking gait. Peak values of ±18 dps indicate normal ambulatory motion.
Heart Rate Over Time
1 Hz · beats per minute (bpm)
PPG
Heart rate recorded by PPG (photoplethysmography). The subject transitions between rest (~55 bpm) and activity (~90 bpm) in a wave-like pattern across the 600-second session.
HRV — SDNN
Heart Rate Variability · ms
HRV
HRV SDNN reflects autonomic nervous system activity. Higher values indicate parasympathetic dominance (rest); lower values occur during stress or exercise. Mean ≈ 45 ms is within a healthy range.
Raw EMG Signal
1000 Hz · mV · 대퇴사두근 (Quadriceps)
RAW
Raw EMG of the quadriceps femoris. The biphasic, zero-mean signal is typical of surface EMG — random noise with embedded motor unit action potentials during contraction.
Rectified EMG
Full-wave rectified · mV
RECTIFIED
Rectified EMG converts the signal to all-positive values by taking absolute values. This is the first step in amplitude envelope extraction, making muscle activation levels easier to quantify.
Skin Temperature
1 Hz · °C
TEMP
Skin temperature fluctuates between 32–34°C. The gradual rise then decline reflects thermoregulatory response to physical activity — vasodilation increases surface temperature during exercise.
Galvanic Skin Response (GSR)
1 Hz · μS (microsiemens)
EDA
GSR / EDA measures electrodermal activity via sweat gland conductance. Rapid transient spikes correspond to sympathetic nervous system arousal events. Mean ~2.4 μS is consistent with moderate arousal.
